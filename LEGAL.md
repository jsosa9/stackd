# Legal Guidelines — stackd

## Priority: HIGH
## Read this before writing any marketing copy, landing page content, ad copy, or user-facing text.

## Scope
These rules apply to the landing page and marketing copy ONLY. The product itself (SMS pipeline, persona generation, coaching conversations) does not need to change. Users drive the product experience by typing names themselves — stackd is not liable for user requests.

---

## The Core Legal Risk

stackd allows users to create AI personas inspired by real public figures. This creates right of publicity, trademark, and false endorsement risks if handled incorrectly in marketing.

The law distinguishes between:
- SAFE: Describing a person's characteristics without naming them in marketing
- SAFE: User types a name themselves inside the product — you are responding to their request, not selling their name
- RISKY: Using a celebrity name in marketing or ads
- RISKY: Implying a celebrity endorses stackd
- ILLEGAL: Using trademarked catchphrases commercially

---

## Rules for Landing Page and Marketing Copy

NEVER do these in any landing page, ad, or marketing:
- Name a specific celebrity, athlete, or public figure
- Show a celebrity photo or likeness
- Use a trademarked phrase
- Imply any celebrity endorses or uses stackd
- Say "chat with Elon Musk" or anything similar

ALWAYS do these instead:
- Use descriptive references without names:
  "The Navy SEAL known for mental toughness"
  "The entrepreneur who built a billion dollar company"
  "The pop star who overcame everything"
- Use generic language that lets the user imagine whoever they want:
  "Text any name. Your coach responds in their style."
  "Pick anyone who inspires you."
  "Your favorite voice. In your texts."
- Let the user supply the name — stackd never names anyone in marketing

---

## Rules for Twilio Registration

When filling out the Twilio toll-free verification:
- Do NOT mention any celebrity names
- Use this description exactly:
  "SMS-based AI accountability coaching app. Users receive daily check-in texts from a personalized AI coach. Users opt in by texting the number first. Message frequency is 1-5 per day. Users can stop at any time by texting STOP."

---

## Rules for Paid Ads (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook)

- Never name a celebrity in ad copy
- Never show a celebrity photo or video
- Use descriptive language only:
  "What if the person who inspires you most texted you every morning?"
  "The coach you always wanted. In your messages."
  "Text any name. Your coach is ready."
- Phone mockups in ads must show fictional names like "Coach Alex" not real celebrity names

---

## The Coaches Carousel (Landing Page)

The carousel must show hints not real names. Describe enough context that users recognize who is meant without naming them directly. This is the legal protection.

---

## Terms of Service (already implemented)

Currently includes:
- stackd is not affiliated with any public figures
- AI coaches are fictional personas inspired by public figures
- Responses do not represent actual views of any real person
- Users are responsible for the names they request

---

## Red Lines — Never Cross These

1. Never use a celebrity name in a paid ad
2. Never use a trademarked catchphrase commercially
3. Never claim affiliation or endorsement
4. Never show a celebrity actual photo
5. Never have the AI claim to be the real person inside marketing materials

---

## Summary

Safe: User types a name inside the product
Safe: Descriptive references in marketing
Safe: Inspired by language inside the product
Risky: Naming celebrities in marketing or ads
Illegal: Trademarked phrases used commercially

When in doubt — describe, do not name.
